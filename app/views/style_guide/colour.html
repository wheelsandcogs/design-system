Colour

Colour helps draw a user’s attention or give focus to an element. It must be used with care and discretion, backed by usability and accessibility testing. In the design system, we use colour to draw attention to actions, links and to the Home Office brand.

Always ask the central design team for advice.

Primary colours

#8F23B3
Purple is the Home Office’s brand colour. It should not be the dominant colour in a digital interface. Overuse can make content hard to read, particularly on services that are used for extended periods of time. Home Office purple works best in digital interfaces when sparingly used as a keyline or accent.
#0B0C0C
Black is used for headings and body content on pages.
#F7F7F7
Light grey is used as the content background colour on internal services to minimise eye strain caused by high contrast user interfaces.

Accessibility

Text and background colors need to meet the minimum contrast ratios specified by Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.0.
